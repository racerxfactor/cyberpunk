AI / UX

Redesigning AI workflows with human-centered MCP.

Enterprise teams needed AI agents that fit naturally into existing workflows — not another tool to learn.

01 — Overview

AI tooling that teams actually use.

The company had deployed several AI-powered agents via the Model Context Protocol, but adoption was stalling. Engineers found the interfaces clunky, context switching was constant, and trust in AI outputs was low. I led the redesign of the entire MCP integration layer — from discovery to delivery.

The goal: make AI agents feel like teammates, not tools.

02 — Problem

Why adoption stalled.

User research across 40+ engineers revealed three friction points:

Context fragmentation

Engineers juggled between their IDE, the AI dashboard, and documentation. Every task required copying context between windows, breaking flow state.

Trust deficit

AI suggestions appeared as black boxes. Without provenance, confidence scores, or edit history, engineers couldn't judge whether to accept or reject outputs.

Rigid interaction model

The existing interface forced a strict prompt → response loop. Real workflows are iterative, branching, and collaborative — the UI didn't support that.

03 — Process

From research to embedded intelligence.

We took a deeply user-centered approach, shadowing engineers for two weeks before sketching a single wireframe.

01

Contextual Inquiry

Shadowed 12 engineers across 3 teams for two weeks. Mapped their actual workflows, tool-switching patterns, and moments of friction with existing AI tools.

02

Interaction Model Redesign

Replaced the rigid prompt/response model with a canvas-based workspace where AI agents could be invoked inline, mid-task, with full project context.

03

Trust Layer Design

Built provenance trails, confidence indicators, and diff-view for every AI output. Engineers could see exactly what the AI changed and why.

04

Iterative Testing

Ran 5 rounds of usability testing with progressively higher-fidelity prototypes. Each round sharpened the interaction patterns.

04 — Solution

Intelligence woven into the developer flow.

The final design embedded MCP agents directly into the IDE sidebar — no context switching. A persistent project context window kept AI grounded in the codebase. Every suggestion came with a confidence score, source trail, and one-click diff view.

The breakthrough feature was "conversational refinement" — engineers could iterate on AI outputs through natural dialogue without re-prompting from scratch, preserving the full chain of reasoning.

What I learned

The hardest part of AI UX isn't making the AI smarter — it's making its intelligence legible. Engineers didn't need more powerful models; they needed to understand and trust the outputs. Transparency was the unlock.
