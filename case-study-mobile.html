Product Design

A wellness app that earns daily use.

Designing a health & wellness companion from zero to 500K downloads — balancing delight, data, and daily habit formation.

01 — Overview

Building a habit, not just an app.

The startup had a strong clinical foundation — validated health protocols, partnerships with nutritionists, and a growing waitlist. What they didn't have was a product that people actually wanted to open every day.

I joined as the founding product designer to take the concept from clinical whitepaper to consumer-grade mobile experience, leading design from 0→1 across iOS and Android.

02 — Problem

The retention cliff.

Early beta data told a clear story:

Day 7 drop-off

78% of users stopped opening the app after the first week. The initial onboarding was thorough but the daily experience felt like homework — logging meals, tracking water, reviewing charts.

Data overload

The beta surfaced every available metric simultaneously. Users couldn't distinguish signal from noise, and the volume of data created anxiety rather than motivation.

Missing emotional hook

Health apps that sustain engagement connect to identity and aspiration, not just metrics. The beta felt clinical — accurate but cold.

Before state
After state

Nobody wakes up wanting to log their water intake. They wake up wanting to feel good about themselves. We need to design for that feeling.

— Rex Cheng, product strategy pitch
03 — Process

From clinical to personal.

The redesign centered on one principle: reduce friction to zero for the daily loop, and make every interaction feel rewarding.

01

User Journey Mapping

Mapped the full emotional arc of a user's first 30 days. Identified the critical "habit loop" moments where engagement either locked in or collapsed.

02

Progressive Disclosure

Redesigned the information architecture to show one insight at a time, unlocking deeper data as users demonstrated engagement. Less became more.

03

Micro-Interaction Design

Crafted 40+ micro-animations for logging, streaks, and milestones. Every tap gave tactile, rewarding feedback — haptics, particle effects, streak celebrations.

04

Social Proof Layer

Added lightweight social features — anonymized cohort progress, shared milestones, and optional accountability partners — without becoming a social network.

04 — Solution

A daily companion that feels good to use.

The redesigned app opens to a single, personalized "Today" card — your one most important action. No dashboards, no charts, no overwhelm. Just one clear thing to do right now.

As users build streaks, the app progressively reveals richer data, deeper insights, and personalized coaching. The information architecture mirrors the user's growing expertise and commitment.

What I learned

The biggest design decision was what to remove, not what to add. Every feature we cut from the first-week experience directly improved retention. Restraint is the hardest skill in consumer product design.
